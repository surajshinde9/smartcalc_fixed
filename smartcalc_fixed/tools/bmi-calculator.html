Understanding Your BMI

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a widely used screening tool that estimates body fat based on height and weight. While it's not a direct measure of body fat percentage, it provides a quick and useful indicator of whether your weight is in a healthy range for your height.

BMI is calculated by dividing your weight in kilograms by the square of your height in meters. The result places you in one of four standard categories used by WHO and health professionals worldwide. However, BMI has limitations — it doesn't account for muscle mass, bone density, age, or ethnic differences.

BMI Categories (WHO Standard)

Underweight: Below 18.5 | Normal weight: 18.5–24.9 | Overweight: 25–29.9 | Obese Class I: 30–34.9 | Obese Class II: 35–39.9 | Obese Class III: 40 and above

BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height (m)²

Example BMI Calculation

Person: Weight = 72 kg, Height = 175 cm (1.75 m)

BMI = 72 ÷ (1.75 × 1.75) = 72 ÷ 3.0625 = 23.5
