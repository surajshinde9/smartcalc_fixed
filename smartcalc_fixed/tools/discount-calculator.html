How Discounts Work

A discount is a reduction in the original price of a product or service. Discounts are expressed either as a percentage off the original price (e.g., 20% off) or as a fixed amount off (e.g., ₹200 off). Understanding how to quickly calculate the final price after a discount helps you make smarter purchasing decisions and avoid being misled by marketing.

Our discount calculator supports three common scenarios: calculating the final price when you know the original price and discount percentage, finding the discount percentage when you know both the original and sale price, and finding how much you save with a fixed discount amount.

Discount Formulas

Final Price = Original Price × (1 - Discount% / 100)
Savings = Original Price × Discount% / 100
Discount% = (Savings / Original Price) × 100

How to Use the Discount Calculator

Example Discount Calculations

Example 1 — 30% off ₹2,499:

Savings = 2499 × 30/100 = ₹749.70 | Final Price = ₹1,749.30

Example 2 — ₹500 flat off ₹3,000:

Discount% = 500/3000 × 100 = 16.7% | Final Price = ₹2,500

Example 3 — Original price if sale price is ₹1,800 after 10% off:

Original = 1800 / (1 - 0.10) = ₹2,000

Frequently Asked Questions

Multiply the original price by 0.20 to find 20% of it (the savings), then subtract from the original. Formula: Final = Original × (1 - 0.20) = Original × 0.80. For example, 20% off ₹1,000 = ₹1,000 × 0.80 = ₹800.
Divide the sale price by (1 - discount%/100). For example, if a product costs ₹850 after a 15% discount: Original = 850 / (1 - 0.15) = 850 / 0.85 = ₹1,000. Use the "Find Original" mode in this calculator.
A stacked discount is when two or more discounts are applied sequentially. For example, 20% off followed by an additional 10% off is NOT 30% off — it's 20% off the original, then 10% off the already-discounted price, equaling 28% total off.
A discount reduces the price upfront — you pay less at the time of purchase. Cashback returns money to you after the purchase (to your wallet, bank, or credit card). Both reduce effective cost, but cashback requires you to pay the full price first.
