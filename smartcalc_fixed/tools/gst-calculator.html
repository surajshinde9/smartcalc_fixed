What is GST and How Does It Work in India?

Goods and Services Tax (GST) is India's comprehensive indirect tax system that replaced multiple cascading taxes like VAT, service tax, and central excise duty. Introduced on July 1, 2017, GST is a unified destination-based tax levied at each stage of the supply chain.

GST in India operates under a dual structure: CGST (Central GST) collected by the central government and SGST (State GST) collected by state governments for intra-state transactions. For inter-state transactions, IGST (Integrated GST) is levied. In most cases, CGST and SGST are each half of the total GST rate.

Understanding GST calculation is essential for businesses to price products correctly and for consumers to verify invoices. The two most common operations are adding GST to a base price (exclusive to inclusive) and removing GST from an MRP to find the base price (inclusive to exclusive).

GST Formulas

Add GST: Final Price = Base Price × (1 + GST%/100)
Remove GST: Base Price = GST-inclusive Price ÷ (1 + GST%/100)

Example GST Calculation

Scenario: Adding 18% GST to ₹1,000

GST Amount = ₹1,000 × 18/100 = ₹180

Final Price = ₹1,000 + ₹180 = ₹1,180

CGST (9%) = ₹90 | SGST (9%) = ₹90

Reverse: Remove 18% GST from ₹1,180

Base Price = ₹1,180 ÷ 1.18 = ₹1,000 | GST = ₹180

Frequently Asked Questions

India has four main GST tax slabs: 5% for essential goods and services, 12% for standard goods like processed food and computers, 18% for most services and goods (the most common rate), and 28% for luxury goods and sin goods. Some items are fully exempt (0% GST).
CGST (Central GST) is collected by the central government and SGST (State GST) by the state, both for intra-state sales — each being half the total GST rate. IGST (Integrated GST) is levied on inter-state sales and imports, and equals the total GST rate, collected by the center and later distributed.
You can search for the applicable GST rate using the HSN (Harmonized System of Nomenclature) code for goods or SAC (Services Accounting Code) for services on the official GST portal (gst.gov.in). Your CA or tax consultant can also help determine the correct rate.
GST is always calculated on the base/exclusive price (price before tax). When you see MRP on products, that is the GST-inclusive price. To find the base price, divide the MRP by (1 + GST rate/100). Our Remove GST function does this automatically.
