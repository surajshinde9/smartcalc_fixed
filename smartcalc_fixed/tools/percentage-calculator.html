Understanding Percentage Calculations

Percentages are one of the most universally useful mathematical concepts — from calculating discounts and tax to understanding growth rates and exam scores. A percentage is simply a fraction out of 100, denoted by the % symbol.

The four calculation modes in this tool cover the most common percentage problems you'll encounter in everyday life. Whether you're calculating how much you'll save in a sale, figuring out what score you got on a test, tracking business growth, or computing GST, percentages are at the heart of it all.

Core Percentage Formulas

X% of Y = (X / 100) × Y
X is ?% of Y = (X / Y) × 100
% Change = ((New - Old) / |Old|) × 100

Example Calculations

1. Find 15% of 2,400: 15/100 × 2,400 = 360

2. 360 is what % of 2,400? 360/2,400 × 100 = 15%

3. Percentage change from 80 to 100: (100–80)/80 × 100 = +25%

4. Add 18% to ₹500: 500 + 18% = 500 + 90 = ₹590

Frequently Asked Questions

Percentage increase = ((New Value - Old Value) / Old Value) × 100. For example, if a price goes from ₹100 to ₹120, the increase = ((120-100)/100) × 100 = 20%. Use the "% Change" tab in this calculator.
Use the formula: (Part / Whole) × 100. For example, 45 out of 60 marks = (45/60) × 100 = 75%. Use the "X is ?% of Y" tab above.
Percentage change is relative: going from 10% to 12% is a 20% increase (2/10 × 100). Percentage points is absolute: the same change is 2 percentage points. These terms are often confused in news and reports. This calculator measures percentage change.
If you know the final value after a percentage was added, divide by (1 + percentage/100) to get the original. E.g., if ₹118 includes 18% GST, original = 118 / 1.18 = ₹100. Our GST calculator automates this for tax calculations.
