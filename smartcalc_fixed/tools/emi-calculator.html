What is EMI and How Does It Work?

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment — a fixed amount you pay every month to repay your loan. It combines both the principal repayment and the interest component into a single convenient payment.

When you take a loan, the bank calculates your EMI based on three factors: the loan amount (principal), the rate of interest, and the loan tenure. In the early months of your loan, a larger portion of your EMI goes towards interest. As you continue paying, the principal component increases and the interest component decreases — this is called loan amortization.

Understanding your EMI helps you plan your monthly budget effectively, compare loan offers from different lenders, and choose a tenure that balances affordability with total interest cost.

EMI Formula

EMI = P × r × (1+r)ⁿ / [(1+r)ⁿ - 1]

Where P = Principal loan amount, r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), n = Number of monthly installments

Example EMI Calculation

Scenario: Home loan of ₹30 Lakhs at 8.5% per annum for 20 years.

Monthly rate (r) = 8.5 / 12 / 100 = 0.007083
Number of months (n) = 20 × 12 = 240

EMI = ₹30,00,000 × 0.007083 × (1.007083)²⁴⁰ / [(1.007083)²⁴⁰ - 1]

= ₹26,035 per month

Total payment = ₹62,48,400 | Total interest = ₹32,48,400
