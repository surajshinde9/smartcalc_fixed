Understanding Loan Repayment

When you take a loan, you agree to repay the principal amount plus interest over a defined period. Most loans use an amortization schedule — each monthly payment covers both interest for that period and a portion of the principal. Early in the loan, most of your payment goes toward interest; later, more goes toward reducing the principal.

The total cost of your loan depends on three key factors: the principal amount (more borrowed = more interest), the interest rate (higher rate = more expensive), and the tenure (longer tenure = lower EMI but much higher total interest). Understanding this relationship helps you make informed borrowing decisions.

Using this loan calculator before applying for any loan gives you a complete picture of what you're committing to — total outgo, interest burden, and monthly impact on your budget.

EMI = P × r × (1+r)ⁿ / [(1+r)ⁿ - 1]

P = Principal | r = Monthly interest rate | n = Total months

Frequently Asked Questions

An amortization schedule is a complete table of periodic loan payments showing the amount of principal and interest that comprise each payment until the loan is paid off at the end of its term. Our calculator shows the first 12 months of your schedule.
Prepaying your loan reduces the outstanding principal, which in turn reduces the interest charged in subsequent months. Even small annual prepayments can significantly reduce total interest paid and shorten the loan tenure. Check if your lender charges prepayment penalties.
A fixed rate loan has the same interest rate throughout the tenure — EMI stays constant. A floating rate loan has an interest rate that changes with market conditions (like RBI repo rate changes). Floating rates are often lower initially but carry the risk of EMI increases.
Shorter tenure = higher EMI but much less total interest. Longer tenure = lower EMI but more interest paid overall. Choose the shortest tenure your monthly budget can comfortably accommodate. As a rule, keep your total EMI obligations below 40% of your monthly income.
