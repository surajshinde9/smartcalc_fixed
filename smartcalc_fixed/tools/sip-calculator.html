What is a SIP and Why Should You Invest?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a disciplined approach to investing in mutual funds. Instead of investing a lump sum, you invest a fixed amount every month. This method leverages the power of compounding — your returns also earn returns over time, leading to exponential wealth growth.

SIP investing also uses rupee cost averaging: you buy more units when prices are low and fewer units when prices are high, automatically averaging your cost. Over long periods, this smooths out market volatility and typically yields better returns than trying to time the market.

Even small monthly amounts, when invested consistently for long durations, can create significant wealth. A ₹5,000/month SIP at 12% returns over 20 years grows to over ₹49 lakhs from just ₹12 lakhs invested — that's over 4× your money through the magic of compounding.

SIP Formula

FV = P × [(1 + r)ⁿ - 1] / r × (1 + r)

Where FV = Future Value, P = Monthly investment, r = Monthly interest rate (annual ÷ 12 ÷ 100), n = Total months

Example SIP Calculation

Monthly SIP: ₹10,000 | Annual Return: 12% | Duration: 15 years

Monthly rate (r) = 12 / 12 / 100 = 0.01 | Months (n) = 15 × 12 = 180

Future Value = ₹10,000 × [(1.01)¹⁸⁰ - 1] / 0.01 × 1.01

= ₹50,45,760

Total Invested: ₹18,00,000 | Wealth Gained: ₹32,45,760 (180% returns!)
